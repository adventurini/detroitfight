Program Overview

Real Boxing.
Not Cardio Kickboxing.

DFC's Boxing & Conditioning program teaches actual boxing fundamentals — footwork, combinations, head movement, defensive technique, and ring IQ — wrapped in conditioning that will change your body composition faster than any treadmill or spin class.

Every session includes structured technical instruction followed by heavy bag work, mitt work, and conditioning circuits. You'll learn how to throw a proper jab, move your feet, and build the kind of functional fitness that combat athletes rely on.

No experience required. Whether you're training for fitness, self-defense, or competition, the program scales to your level from day one.

What You'll Learn

Skills Breakdown

  • Stance, footwork, and ring movement
  • Jab, cross, hook, uppercut — proper form and power generation
  • Defensive skills — slips, rolls, parries, and head movement
  • Heavy bag technique and combinations
  • Mitt work with coaches
  • Conditioning circuits designed for fighters

All Levels Welcome — No prior experience needed. Every class is coached and scaled to your ability.

What to Bring: Comfortable workout clothes, a water bottle, and a towel. Hand wraps recommended (available for purchase at the gym). Loaner gloves available while you get started.

Program Overview

Complete Striking.
Not Just Kickboxing.

Muay Thai is the most versatile striking art in combat sports. At DFC, you'll learn the full system — punches, kicks, elbows, knees, and clinch work — in a structured curriculum that builds power, timing, and fight IQ from session one.

Every class combines technical instruction with pad work, partner drills, and live application. Your coaches don't just demonstrate technique — they break down the timing, distance, and setups that make it work against a resisting partner.

Whether you're looking for a complete striking foundation, cross-training for Jiu-Jitsu, or preparing to compete, the Muay Thai program has you covered.

What You'll Learn

Skills Breakdown

  • Full striking arsenal — punches, kicks, elbows, knees
  • Thai pad work and partner drills
  • Clinch technique — sweeps, knees, off-balancing
  • Defensive footwork and timing
  • Fight IQ — reading opponents, setting up attacks
  • Conditioning built into every session

All Levels Welcome — Beginners train alongside experienced students. Coaches scale instruction to your level.

What to Bring: Comfortable workout clothes, a water bottle, and a towel. Shin guards recommended for partner drills (available for purchase at the gym). Loaner gear available while you get started.

Our Coaches

Every coach at DFC is an active practitioner. They don't just teach — they train, they compete, and they lead by example.

Sean Goolsby

Head Coach & Co-Owner

Jiu-Jitsu

Sean didn't learn Jiu-Jitsu in Michigan. He learned it in Manaus, Brazil — training under brothers Cristiano and Angelo Carioca, competing within his first three months, and earning his blue and purple belts in one of the most serious training environments in the Amazon.

The path led to São Paulo and nearly seven years under Claudio Calasans, a multiple-time World Champion and one of the most complete grapplers alive. That's where Sean earned his brown and black belts. That's where he became a professional.

From São Paulo to Hungary, training under Max Carvalho and winning the 2022 European IBJJF No-Gi Championship at black belt Ultra Heavy in his international debut. Then to the UAE — competing for Baniyas Club, teaching under Palm Sports, and competing across the AJP Tour through Europe and North Africa. By season's end he was ranked #1 No-Gi in the World and #4 Overall on the AJP Tour.

Back in Michigan in late 2023, he connected with David Garmo and Assembly Jiu-Jitsu — a partnership that sharpened his no-gi game to another level.

His Record

Pan American IBJJF Champion. European IBJJF No-Gi Champion. Ranked #1 No-Gi in the World. Multiple IBJJF gold medals across four continents in gi and no-gi, adult and masters. ADCC competitor including the American Trials. Still actively competing.

Why Detroit Fight Company

Jiu-Jitsu gave Sean direction when he needed it, discipline when it was hard, and a community that spanned the world. Detroit Fight Company exists to do the same for others — not just to teach technique, but to change lives. That's been the mission from day one.

DFC Winter Springs is an official affiliate of Esporte Dez / Instituto Calasans Camargo. The lineage is real. The door is open.

From Detroit to the world. Now rooted in Orlando.

Pat Washington

Head Boxing Coach

Boxing

Pat Washington is the heart and soul of Detroit Fight Company's boxing program. A former professional boxer with a 20-1 record and 15 knockouts, Pat captured the WBF Intercontinental Light Welterweight Championship in just his ninth professional fight — displaying the technical skill and two-handed power that had observers calling him one of the best-kept secrets in American boxing.

Before turning pro, Pat served as a paratrooper in the U.S. Army's 82nd Airborne Division at Fort Bragg, where he represented the base as a competitive boxer. His professional career took him from the small-show circuit through the Carolinas to a main-event title fight, where he earned a clear unanimous decision for the belt.

But Pat's legacy extends far beyond his own career in the ring. He has dedicated decades to developing young boxers — not just as fighters, but as people. At DFC, Pat brings a lifetime of experience, discipline, and genuine care for every athlete who steps through the door.

Karl

Boxing Coach

Boxing

Karl discovered combat sports at the age of nine, introduced by his father, and it didn't take long for him to fall in love with boxing. With over 11 years in the sport and 15 fights under his belt — both as a competitor and a corner coach — boxing has become a fundamental part of his life.

Karl's passion is sharing the sport with others. Whether you're lacing up gloves for the first time or sharpening your skills for competition, his coaching is built on the belief that boxing transforms people from the inside out.

Hana Radi

Women's Program Coach

Jiu-Jitsu

Hana Radi began her Jiu-Jitsu journey in 2020, initially looking for a way to break up the routine of weight training. It didn't take long to realize she had found something much deeper. Jiu-Jitsu challenged her mentally and physically in ways she had never experienced, and she quickly understood that growth in this sport comes down to one thing: consistency.

Through training, Hana has learned to face discomfort head-on — whether that means stepping onto the competition mats or pushing through tough sessions. As a brown belt competitor, she leads DFC's Women's Only Gi Jiu-Jitsu program with a coaching philosophy built on the belief that iron sharpens iron. Her classes are challenging, supportive, and designed for women of all experience levels.

John Venturini

Co-Owner

No-Gi Jiu-Jitsu

In 2021, John stepped onto the mats for the first time at 290 lbs with a simple conviction: if he was going to ask his kids to compete in combat sports, he had to be willing to do it himself.

What started as leading by example became a complete transformation. Over 100 lbs lost, a competition purple belt earned, and a growing collection of IBJJF hardware — including gold at No-Gi American Nationals, silver at the Pan-American No-Gi Championships, and two bronze medals at the No-Gi World Championships.

But the medals aren't the point. John's real passion is coaching kids. In a world of endless screens and manufactured comfort, he believes combat sports offer something irreplaceable — real challenge, real consequence, real growth. The kind of resilience that can't be downloaded. He started this journey to show his own children what was possible. Now he's Detroit Fight Company's biggest cheerleader — and the guy making sure every kid who walks through the door gets that same opportunity.

Luis

Kids Jiu-Jitsu Coach

Jiu-Jitsu

Wrestling took over Luis's life at 15 years old. After graduating high school with college wrestling scholarships in hand, he chose a different path — four years of service in the United States Marines, where he became a shooting instructor and rose to the rank of Sergeant.

After the military, Luis found Jiu-Jitsu and earned his blue belt while competing across multiple organizations — winning an IBJJF Open, two NAGAs, and a New Breed tournament, with additional podium finishes along the way. He's also spent the past year sharpening his boxing alongside the DFC coaching staff.

Luis brings a wrestler's work ethic and a Marine's discipline to coaching the next generation. He also serves as a high school wrestling coach, and at DFC he channels that experience into developing young athletes through the kids Jiu-Jitsu program.
